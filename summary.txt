The Insurance Risk & Claims Analysis dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of policy distribution, customer demographics,
and claim patterns across approximately 30.1K insurance policies, with a total claim amount of about 150M and an average claim amount of around 5K.
The gender distribution of policyholders is nearly balanced, with approximately 15.1K female and 15K male customers.
Most policies are associated with private vehicle use (around 30K), while a smaller portion relates to commercial vehicles (about 7K). In terms of vehicle brands,
Ford leads with about 2.7K policies, followed by Chevrolet (2.3K) and other manufacturers such as Dodge, Toyota, and GMC.
Age group analysis shows that the majority of policies fall within the 26–65 age range, each group holding roughly 5.6K–5.7K policies,
while younger drivers (15–25) represent a smaller share. Coverage zones are fairly evenly distributed across urban, suburban, rural, highly urban,
and highly rural areas, each contributing close to 6K policies, indicating balanced geographic coverage.
The analysis of vehicle manufacturing years shows policy growth from the early 1990s to the mid-2010s,
peaking around 2016–2018, followed by a noticeable decline in the most recent year of data. Education-level insights reveal that Bachelor’s
degree holders account for the largest proportion of policies (about 15K), followed by High School (~8K) and Master’s (~5K),
with a smaller segment holding PhDs. Additionally, most policyholders have no kids driving (around 21K), while households with one or 
more young drivers form a significantly smaller share. The dataset fields—including customer demographics, vehicle information,
claim frequency, claim amount, household income, and family characteristics—support detailed risk profiling and help insurers identify segments 
associated with higher claim frequencies and financial exposure, enabling better decision-making in underwriting and policy management.
